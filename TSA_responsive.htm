WHO WE ARE

Since 1991, High Technology High School has been a chapter of TSA. It has undergone many leadership changes since then. As of the 2016-2017 school year, our chapter is home to 150 members, which comprises over 50% of the school's population. With students possessing immense talent and drive, there's no doubt that HTHS has been an extremely active member of the Technology Student Association...sending students to the State and National Conferences annually. In addition, we are proud to send students to become state officers as well, thereby continuing to spread the love of technological learning throughout NJ TSA.

HTHS TSA: VEX

The VEX Robotics Competition (VRC) is a robotics event for high schoolers. Teams build robots to complete an annual challenge. For the 2016-2017 season, the VRC challenge is STARSTRUCK, in which two alliances, composed of 2 teams each, earn points by scoring stars and cubes in designated zones in their respective zones and by hanging their robots on the Hanging Bar. This is the second year that the 5-member HTHS team, Team 765A, competed in the VRC challenge. Also, this is the first year that a secondary HTHS VEX team, Team 765X, competed at the regional and state levels. They won the most prestigious award available, the Excellence Award, at their regional competition, where they were also tournament finalists. This qualified them for the state-level competition. These awards qualify them for the international level of competition, VEX Worlds, where they will be competing in mid-April.

What are your TSA responsibilities / title?
I'm the vice president of our chapter. I help with running the club and overseeing its activities - this involves attending meetings, organizing workshops for members, and generally offering assistance wherever I or my fellow officers see fit.

How long have you been a part of TSA?
I've been a member of TSA since seventh grade, which makes six years now!

What competitions have you participated in / are going to participate in?
Throughout my TSA career I've participated in a variety of competitions, from System Control Technology to Technology Bowl to Debating Technological Issues to VEX Robotics.

What is your favorite part about TSA?
I really appreciate the variety of events available for members to participate in, but I'm also amazed by the strong sense of community TSA creates between participants. I was first drawn to TSA by the opportunity to try my hand at a variety of different events, all connected by the common thread of STEM experience and leadership. But I stayed in TSA because I realized I had forged new friendships with incredible people as a member. TSA creates a sense of community and engenders strong relationships between people who might have nothing in common besides the fact that they both belong to this incredible organization. I'm very proud to be a part of it.

Why do you do TSA?
I'm excited about what the future holds for me, and I can't wait to get out into the world and tackle real, tangible problems. But until then, I want to get as much experience as I can in as many areas as I can -- and TSA is one of the best places to do that. Where else can I simultaneously indulge my interest in robotics and also hone my public speaking skills? Where else are aerodynamics and fashion design considered equally interesting and valuable? TSA gives me the opportunity to sharpen my technical skills while meeting bright and interesting people. TSA is a community I belong to, and gives me a sense of home. TSA is practically part of who I am -- so I can't really help doing it, can I?

What are some chapter activities (past or present or future)?
Currently, our chapter is involved with fundraising for the American Cancer Society through the Relay for Life program. We're currently gearing up for our state conference, so that involves a good deal of logistical work on our end as officers. We're also preparing materials for our chapter members on the newly introduced LEAP program, and trying to make the transition as easy as possible for our members.
